Around 2005 or 2006 I discovered org-mode for [emacs](https://www.gnu.org/software/emacs/) . I was my sole reason for moving from Vim to emacs. For the last approximately 19 years I have used emacs as my daily driver for note taking. It took me a solid month to get comfortable using emacs. As anyone who has used it will tell you there is a huge learning curve. However once you "get it" you end up wanting to use it for nearly everything. 

I used Org-Mode to organize my thoughts. I used emacs to send and receive email. That only lasted for a day or two. I have used it on and off for calendering, playing mp3's, listening to Shoutcast streams, RoamNotes, and as an RSS reader and web browser. Oh, and yes I used it as my IDE.

That is until I found [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/) 

I found Obsidian to be the best note taking application I have come across. The follow the philosophy of "future proof your work". What does that mean? Just like my beloved emacs, they use plain text files. Your notes although they will be displayed and entered in a beautiful manner, are nothing more than old fashioned ASCII files. ASCII files will be able to be read for the next 10,000 years. 

This website was cobbled together using Obsidian and a plugin called Quartz (see the footer for the link). This site is hosted on Github and when my changes are pushed up to Git, the site gets deployed automatically. 

Obsidian used as a blog publishing platform is cool. However where it really shines is taking notes and helping to organize your thoughts. To really get an understanding of what Obsidian is check out this YouTube Playlist [Obsidian for Beginners](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL3NaIVgSlAVLHty1-NuvPa9V0b0UwbzBd)
