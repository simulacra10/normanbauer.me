    Water Baptism

    Apr 29, 2024, 6 min read

    Today Russel Brand is getting baptized. As such the world of Protestantism has plenty to say on the matter. One such comment on the matter is below.

    Someone commented this on the post above:

    Baptism Means nothing if He has not placed his faith in the finished work of Jesus Christ and repented of his sins. If he has, then praise God. I love Russell Brand, one of the smartest and funniest guys out there.

    Friend, you have this backwards.

    James 2:14: What shall it profit, my brethren, if a man say he hath faith, but hath not works? Shall faith be able to save him?

    James 2:15: And if a brother or sister be naked and want daily food: James 2:16: And one of you say to them: Go in peace, be ye warmed and filled; yet give them not those things that are necessary for the body, what shall it profit?

    James 2:17: So faith also, if it have not works, is dead in itself.

    If you are a Bible believing Christian like me, then you have to realize that what our friend professed to believe is not what the Bible teaches.

    And then there was this comment:

    Water does not wash away our sins. the blood of Jesus alone does that.

    And then later this:

    I am a Bible believing Christian. If you say baptism can wash away sin then why would Almighty God send his Son to die? Please. You are taking scripture out of context. The thief on the cross was not baptized. I encourage baptism but to say it is necessary for salvation takes away from what Jesus did on the cross.

    Acts shows us that there is more than one thing required for salvation.

    1. Witness and acceptance. Phillip witnesses to the eunuch. The eunuch hears and accepts.
    2. The eunuch is Baptized.
    3. Our sins are in fact washed away by baptism.
